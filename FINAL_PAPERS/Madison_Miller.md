## Final Project: A Community-Led Directory for UIUC and the Greater Champaign-Urbana Community
### Abstract
Throughout this paper, I will propose community led-directory designed to support UIUC students and members of the greater Champaign-Urbana community in locating essential resources for basic needs. The directory will include several categories from housing food resources to career and legal services. Using the structure of open-source principles, this resource will begin as a GitHub-hosted dataset which will be used to power a WordPress website, social media channels, and email updates to the public. All in all, this project proposal will demonstrate how open-source project management can be applied to sustainably benefit community information infrastructure.

### Introduction 
Finding basic resources is often presented as a question of availability and community involvement, when in reality, the true struggle is navigating sea of disorganized and inaccurate information. A majority of the time, there are few physical barriers to access food assistance, healthcare services, and housing support, but students and community members still struggle to find accurate information on these resources in a timely manner. Information about eligibility, hours, and costs is scattered across outdated websites, word-of-mouth conversations, flyers and social media posts. As a result, individuals who need support may spend hours to find one reliable organization they can turn to, and those who need immediate care may become helpless in crisis situations.

As an information sciences student, I have learned how powerful the role of information is in shaping the infrastructure of a community. When the resources that are supposed to keep a community informed are incomplete, outdated, or difficult to navigate, it has the same effects of resource scarcity, which can amplify the preexisting inequalities that are present within a community. The goal of this project is to minimize these barriers and amplify the voices of organizations who may not be able to afford to keep up website maintenance and consistently post on their social media pages. This project has intentionally included both UIUC students and the surrounding Champaign-Urbana community in acknowledgement of the fact there are distinct gaps in the ways that resources are shared within the campus-community ecosystem. Embracing this interconnectedness is key to preserving the university’s surrounding community.

The following will outline the design, management, and sustainability of this community-led directory which is proposed using open-source principles. Each section interconnects key concepts and best practices for project management from IS 340 in order to address the operations and long term viability of the directory. It is my hope that this paper will also bring awareness to the fact that informational gaps are not accidental, and must be addressed by correcting structural failures and reusing balancing institutional power.

### Project Overview
This proposed project is a publicly accessible directory of essential resources that are available to UIUC students and Champaign-Urbana residents. The directory will explicitly state if certain resources are limited to students. It is designed to be easy to navigate, regularly updated and dependent on the real experiences of community members. The primary function of this tool is to help users quickly identify the resources that are relevant to their situation with clear and tangible next steps about how to access them. Target resources are primarily free or low-cost. Accessibility will be categorized into eligibility requirements, locations, hours of operation, and costs if applicable. 

Structurally, the project will separate internal data management from the public-facing digital products. The official structured database will be hosted on GitHub and will allow maintainers to track changes in real time, review updates, and detailed version histories. Public facing platforms will include a WordPress website, social media accounts (primarily Instagram and Facebook), and email communications. Each of these public-facing resources will pull from the dataset and present information in accessible formats. This separation is important to maintaining the integrity of the data, creating realistic access points, and keeping to the open-source principles of the project.

Community participation is another key component to the design of this project. Users and maintainers will be encourages to submit new resources, flag outdated information, and suggest further details through both structured forms and online community discussion spaces. Each submission that is submitted will be carefully reviewed by maintainers before being added to the dataset prioritizing both accuracy and community knowledge-sharing. This project seeks to include several categories for basic needs in a way that is both manageable for maintainers and addressing several areas of impact.

### Defining the Problem
It is important to note that the core problem that this project addresses it not a lack of reliable resources, but a lack of maintained information about the plethora of resources that are available to the community. In the UIUC and greater CU area, there are many organizations that offer community services. However, the information about about these services is fragmented across multiple platforms and mediums. This fragmentation disproportionately affects those who are new to the area or may lack the digital literacy to scour the internet for these resources themselves. In addition to the issue of access, the details of these resources and organizations are likely to undergo frequent changes in the form of staffing changes, funding cycles, and changes in eligibility requirements. Without a system that is designed to keep up with these changes, websites and static directories will quickly become outdated and misleading. Finally exacting systems for distributing resource information fail to include community members as active participants. If there’s no clear way for community members or organizations themselves to contribute, it breaks down the trust between community organizations and third-parties that claim to help promote them. These issues are especially prevalent in student-led initiatives, as students a) may lack the ability to facilitate understanding and collaboration with the CU community on a fundamental level or b) are constantly rotating in and out of the area casing high-turnover and wavering levels of commitment.

### Types of Resources
The directory is intentionally organized to provide a defined set of resource categories for the most common and most pressing needs shared by UIUC students and members of the CU community. Organizing the project by category allows for an improved user experience and a sustainable system for maintenance. Each category will include resources from which the user can sort by date added,are frequently accessed, or time-sensitive allowing them to navigate the site independently and intuitively.

The food category includes food pantries, meal programs, grocery assistance initiatives, and campus or community food distribution events. Entries in this category will include an interactive map and prioritize clear information about eligibility, distribution schedules, and distance. Health resources include free or low-cost clinics, mental health services, and wellness programs that are available both on and off campus. Housing resources will focus on providing short term assistance, housing navigation services, tenant support, and emergency shelter information, rather than private rental listings. Ensuring that these categories are regularly audited and updates is essential to keeping the project useful and reliable

Technology resources will include device lending programs, free or low-cost internet access, and digital literacy support. Being able to not only access, but also understand technology is imperative to academic success, employment, and researching other resources. The next category, legal services, will include free or low-cost legal aid, tenant advocacy, immigration support, and student-focused legal services. Career resources will provide information for resume review, interview prep, and workforce-development programs that are relevant to both students and community members. Finally, the events category will highlight community programs, workshops, and gatherings that serve as opportunities for connection, skill building, or informational sessions for any of the available resources that are listed.

Each resource entry will follow a consistent structure including:
The name of the service or organization
Eligibility
Location, address, or access method
Hours of operation
Forms of contact (phone, email, social media if applicable)
Important events, dates, or deadlines
The date of the last verification of the resource

### Community
I. Community Composition <br>
The project is structured around strengthening the shared community that includes UIUC students, Champaign-Urbana residents, and local organizations. To facilitate understanding and shared accountability, it is important to treat these groups as interconnected participants rather than separate audiences. Recognizing the geographic and social overlap of these demographics is essential to designing a directory that is reflective of how these groups actually seek our and interact with support systems. Community organizations are a critical part the ecosystem and their input will ensure that the resource information that is distributed is accurate and reflects the intentions and missions that are unique to each organization.

II. Community Participation <br>
Community participation at the core of this project going beyond passive use of the directory. Users are encouraged participate by submitting a form to add new resources, flagging outdated or inaccurate information, and suggesting any feedback that they may have about the site. Submission pathways are integrated into the interface allowing individuals to share information or opinions without having to be necessarily tech savvy. In addition to the flagship site, it’s important to maintain community discussion spaces including social media platforms, potentially a Facebook group, to create opportunities for casual dialogue and share updates in real time. A model that centers participation is important to allow those who rely on these resources to have the most up-to-date information possible. Through this implementation, the project is able to benefit from community knowledge while still maintaining a clear process for resource verification.

III. Community Governance <br>
The governance model for this project implements a soft structure that ensures that all parties are able to contribute and are also held accountable for their contributions. Defining clear roles and responsibilities within the project is designed to accommodate student turnover and changing availability. These roles include maintainers who are responsible for reviewing submissions and managing releases, contributors who submit updates or new resources, outreach coordinators who facilitate engagement with community organizations, and project managers who are in charge of a set number of resource categories.

Documentation is also key to the governance of this project. It is imperative that contribution templates/guidelines, review standards/procedures, and workflow descriptions are defined at the beginning of this project. Documentation should also consider that nw contributors will be stepping into these roles every semester, so it is important that documentation prioritizes the transfer of knowledge and echoes the core values  of the project. Decision making authority should also be clearly outlined so that participants know who to go to when they run into issues or want to connect an organization to the project.

### Risk Management
There are three main risks to consider when understanding the management approach for this project:

Contributor turnover will always be an ongoing concern, as student involvement is a key part of this project. To mitigate this risk, it is important to emphasize documentation, as outlined in the Community Governance section of this paper, and sync maintenance cycles key ending and starting dates within the academic school year. Semester-based audits in addition to regular maintenance is an opportunity for knowledge to be transferred and reviewed as new students join the initiative.

Funding instability is another significant challenge for this project. For the sake of longevity and upkeep,  is important not to rely on complex or costly infrastructure. This project outlines the use of widely available tools and prioritizes low-operational costs. This approach allows the directory to remain functional even during times when funding is limited or irregular. When possible partnerships, volunteers, and university support can serve as supplementary efforts.

Finally, it is important to consider the risks of platform dependency. Designating GitHub as a host for our dataset and WordPress and Social platforms for presentation and communication avoids over-reliance on one single public facing tool. If one platform becomes unavailable unexpectedly, the project data and workflow will remain intact and there will be alternative options for communication to fall back on.

### Workflows
I. Submission <br>
Resource updates enter tye system through structured submissions via an embedded google forms capability on the website. Submissions may also come from direct community and university outreach or facilitated discussions with organizations. These pathways will allow for individuals and organizations alike to contribute information through a combination of structured and organic intake processes.

II. Review <br>
Submissions are then reviewed by maintainers to verify the accuracy, relevance, and completeness of each source. Criteria for review will prioritize clarity of eligibility, current availability, and alignment with the project’s defined resource categories.

III. Release <br>
Approved updates are added to the GitHub dataset and published through public-facing platforms on a consistent schedule. Release communications highlight significant changes to reinforce transparency with the public and trust within the community.

### Tools
The tools chosen for this project are intentionally simple, familiar, and widely supported. This directory is community-led and student-maintained, so the technical infrastructure needs to prioritize accessibility and continuity. The goal is not to build a technically advanced system. The goal set in place processes that can be easily understood, adopted, and maintained by new contributors over time.

GitHub serves as the host for all resource data. Hosting the dataset on GitHub allows maintainers to track changes, review edits, and maintain a clear history of updates. This structure supports accountability and transparency while making it easier to audit and correct information as needed. GitHub issues and pull requests can also be used to flag outdated resources or propose additions, creating a clear workflow for collaboration.

WordPress is the primary public-facing platform for the directory. It presents the information stored in the GitHub dataset in a way that is accessible to a broad audience, making sure to consider individuals with limited technical experience. WordPress is well suited for this role because it supports clear navigation, searchable categories, and structured pages while remaining relatively easy to manage and update. It’s important to note that, WordPress is an implementation meant to display these resources, not to serve as any kind of data authority.

Social media platforms, particularly Instagram and Facebook, are used to increase visibility and engagement. These platforms allow the project to meet community members where they already are and to share updates in real time. A potential Facebook group can also function as an informal forum where users discuss resources, ask questions, and surface gaps that may not yet be reflected in the directory. Email updates serve as a more formal communication channel, allowing the project to share periodic summaries of updates, newly added resources, or upcoming audits.

### Project Timeline
I. Phase 1: Planning <br>
Resource categories are finalized, contribution guidelines are written, and the GitHub dataset is structured. The WordPress site is set up, and core documentation is created to support future contributors.

II. Phase 2: Collaboration <br>
Resources are added through submissions, outreach to campus and community organizations, and user feedback. Workflows for submission and review are tested and refined, and contributor roles are formalized.

III. Phase 3: Implementation <br>
Release schedules are standardized, semester-based audits are implemented, and communication channels are used consistently to share updates. The directory operates as a maintained system.

### Conclusion
This project demonstrates how a community-led directory can function as sustainable information infrastructure when supported by intentional project management and open-source principles. While the project is intentionally outlined to fit UIUC and the greater Champaign-Urbana community, its structure allows for future adaptation, if there’s interest. The ability to replicate this model in other college towns is an opportunity to strengthen connections between campuses and their surrounding communities.
