## Abstract
Black women in the United States experience disproportionately high maternal mortality rates, a disparity driven by a lack of transparency within the medical system and systemic racism. While there is existing data and technological tools that show this trend, they fail to address the underlying issues contributing to the high mortality rates for Black women. These systems do not promote accountability or offer meaningful solutions to the root causes of the problem.
This paper proposes BetterMotherhood, a hypothetical open-source digital platform designed to address Black maternal mortality and medical racism through transparent data aggregation, community initiatives, and ethical technology management. This project integrates publicly available maternal health data related to Black women with personalized information, either anonymized or non-anonymized, to encourage advocacy, research, reform, solution development, and policy change.
This paper further examines how BetterMotherhood would be managed using open-source principles and how it follows the five pillars of open source, including publicly available source code, copyright licensing, free distribution and remixing, and technological neutrality. In addition, this project outlines the proposed solution, target audience, project phases, implementation strategy, licensing, community engagement, and technical stack.

## Introduction
Maternal mortality and medical racism remain persistent health crises in the United States. However, Black women experience significantly higher rates of pregnancy-related complications and death. Studies from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) show that Black women are more likely to die during childbirth than their White counterparts. This issue occurs regardless of income level or educational background, highlighting that these disparities go beyond individual attributes and are deeply systemic. These outcomes also reflect the medical racism that Black women face within the healthcare system, from not being offered pain medication when they are clearly in pain due to the belief that Black women tolerate pain better, to their concerns simply not being believed or taken seriously.
A major pain point surrounding this project is the lack of clarity and accessibility of data related to Black maternal mortality and medical racism. While this data exists, it is often fragmented, not cohesive, difficult to access, and lacks sufficient depth. Additionally, many of the technologies used within healthcare to address maternal health issues are not public-facing and fail to capture the lived realities of Black mothers. Because of this, the healthcare system is unable to accurately identify and address the needs of Black women, which ultimately prevents the development of tangible and effective solutions to this crisis.
A potential solution to this issue is the creation of an open-source system that offers an alternative approach centered on collaboration, transparency, free access, and shared ownership. By making the project’s source code, decision-making processes, and system operations publicly available, this platform would invite participation from a diverse group of contributors, most importantly, Black women. This is especially important because human-centered design is essential when creating platforms meant to serve marginalized communities. Designing with users in mind allows communities to better understand the systemic issues they face and actively contribute to solutions that address those challenges.
With this in mind, this paper introduces BetterMotherhood, a hypothetical open-source digital platform that aims to address Black maternal mortality and medical racism through community engagement, transparent data aggregation, and ethical technology implementation. This paper will explore the project’s target audience, goals, platform design, and use of the five pillars of open source, as well as its implementation strategy. Ultimately, BetterMotherhood seeks to demonstrate how open-source projects can be leveraged to promote accessibility, accountability, and equity in Black women’s maternal healthcare.

## Purpose
The purpose of this project is to propose BetterMotherhood, a hypothetical open-source digital platform designed to address Black maternal mortality and medical racism through community engagement, transparency, and accurate data. This project aims to demonstrate how open-source principles can be integrated into healthcare-related issues in order to address maternal mortality and medical racism affecting Black women.
Additionally, this project aims to encourage others to create open-source projects for issues they believe would benefit from open collaboration, whether related to public health, safety, or other social concerns. Through BetterMotherhood, this project seeks to inspire individuals and communities to use open-source platforms as tools for advocacy, education, and systemic change.
This project also aims to improve accessibility by providing readily available information for first-time mothers and mothers in general. By doing so, BetterMotherhood seeks to help women build community, prepare for motherhood, and feel supported throughout their maternal journey. Ensuring accountability within healthcare systems is another central goal of this project.
This paper also seeks to explore how BetterMotherhood would be managed as an open-source project by examining its licensing, technical framework, governance structure, and overall management approach.

## Target Audience
The target audience for this project is Black women who are seeking a platform to learn more about their options regarding prenatal, antenatal, and childcare in general. There are many things that people who are trying to get pregnant should be doing long before pregnancy, however, this information is not widely known. Even when it is mentioned, it often gets buried under large amounts of other information. Because of this, the primary target audience is Black women who want access to all of this information in one centralized and accessible space.
Another key part of the target audience includes Black women who may have experienced medical racism and want to better understand whether what they experienced was valid. For those who have faced medical racism, this platform aims to help them explore their options moving forward and identify possible next steps.
Additionally, the platform will serve as a space for donating baby clothes that are no longer needed. It will also allow Black women to share information about medical professionals or hospitals where they felt well cared for, as well as share experiences with hospitals or medical professionals who were unwelcoming or dismissive. This platform will also be used to discuss topics such as IVF, adoption, schooling, and other subjects related to motherhood.
While this platform is open for anyone to use regardless of race or gender, Black women are strongly encouraged to contribute, as the platform is designed primarily for them. Others are welcome to participate as long as their contributions do not overshadow Black voices and remain respectful and supportive of the community.

## Proposed Solution
The proposed solution to addressing Black maternal mortality and medical racism is the development of BetterMotherhood, an open-source digital platform designed to promote transparency, foster community among Black women, and centralize maternal health information. The platform is intended to address and fill gaps within the current healthcare system by creating a space where Black women can access reliable information, share experiences, encourage and support one another, and engage with resources related to pregnancy, childbirth, and motherhood.
An important aspect of BetterMotherhood is its focus on transparent and accessible maternal health data. To support this goal, the platform would provide reliable, publicly available maternal health data related to Black maternal outcomes and present it in a way that is easy to understand and navigate. Making this information accessible increases awareness of existing disparities while also encouraging accountability within the healthcare system. This approach allows users to better understand the risks and realities associated with pregnancy and helps them feel more prepared to advocate for themselves, whether during pregnancy or when visiting healthcare facilities in general.
In addition to data access, BetterMotherhood aims to promote community-based support. The platform creates a space where Black women can share personal experiences related to prenatal care, antenatal care, childbirth, postpartum care, and interactions with medical professionals. These shared experiences can serve as validation for individuals who may have experienced medical racism, while also helping others avoid similar situations. Beyond childbirth, the platform can also be used to discuss adoption, IVF, parenting, and other aspects of motherhood.
Open-source principles are essential to this proposal because operating as an open-source platform encourages collaboration, transparency, and shared ownership, all of which give users greater control over the platform. Making BetterMotherhood open source is especially important because medical racism and maternal mortality are sensitive topics. Users need to trust that their experiences are being shared through a platform that is not owned by private companies, where there is a risk of data being sold or misused. Open-source development helps address concerns related to privacy, transparency, and trust, which are critical when engaging with such personal and vulnerable experiences.
Finally, to ensure accessibility for users who may not be technologically savvy, the platform would include a blog-style feature where users can share experiences in a more familiar and user-friendly format. Additionally, private messaging options would be available for individuals who may not feel comfortable sharing their experiences publicly. These features help strengthen community building and ensure that all users feel supported and included.

##Implementation Strategy, Workflow, and Phases
The implementation of BetterMotherhood will follow a four-phase agile workflow approach to encourage flexibility and transparency. This approach allows the platform to continue to grow over time based on community feedback while maintaining an organized and open-source development process. The implementation is structured around four key phases: Define, Design, Development, and Production.
## Phase One: Definition and User Needs Assessment
The implementation process begins with a definition phase focused on understanding user needs and identifying the core challenges related to Black maternal mortality and medical racism. This phase includes both primary and secondary research methods such as surveys, flyers posted in predominantly Black spaces, and community discussions on social media platforms like Instagram and Reddit. It also involves interviews and collecting feedback from Black women and individuals involved in maternal health and addressing medical racism. The goal of this phase is to ensure the platform is grounded in real experiences and actual community needs.
## Phase Two: Design and Workflow Planning
During the design phase, insights gathered from the definition phase are translated into platform workflows. This stage remains flexible to support the agile methodology, allowing changes to be made as new information is learned. Maintaining flexibility at this stage ensures that the platform design continues to align with user needs as they evolve.
## Phase Three: Agile Development and Prototyping
This phase follows an agile development approach, meaning features are built iteratively through prototyping rather than all at once. Development takes place in a public GitHub repository, allowing contributors to view the code, report concerns, and suggest changes. Gradually adding updates helps with testing features and incorporating user feedback into future iterations, making the platform more responsive and effective.
## Phase Four: Production and Ongoing Iteration
Once the core features are implemented and reach a stable state, the platform moves into production with an initial public release. GitHub continues to be used to manage updates, track issues, and support collaboration after launch. This is necessary because even in production, BetterMotherhood is intended to remain an evolving platform with regular updates influenced by ongoing user feedback.

## Technology Used: 
To support the implementation of BetterMotherhood as an open-source and accessible platform, the following technologies will be used.
## Frontend: 
The frontend of BetterMotherhood would be built using React. React enables the platform to provide an accessible user experience across all devices, including mobile phones, which is important given that many users may rely on mobile access.
## Backend: 
The backend would be developed using Java. Java is widely used due to its reliability and scalability, making it suitable for supporting the platform’s core functionality.
## Database: 
BetterMotherhood could use MySQL or MariaDB to store structured data such as user submissions and platform content.
## Data Visualization and Content Management:
To display maternal health data using visualizations, libraries such as Chart.js or D3.js would be used to help present information in a clear and understandable way.
## Accessibility and Security Considerations:
Accessibility is an important priority for BetterMotherhood. To ensure the platform is usable by individuals with diverse needs, automated accessibility testing tools such as Google Lighthouse would be used throughout development.

## Technical Debt & Sustainability:
Technical debt is expected during the early stages of a project. However, BetterMotherhood aims to actively address technical debt, as unmanaged technical debt can make a platform difficult to update and maintain over time. By addressing technical debt early, the platform will be able to grow alongside its community in a more sustainable way.
One way BetterMotherhood would manage technical debt is by maintaining consistent and clear documentation. All documentation related to BetterMotherhood would be stored in the GitHub repository and updated to reflect any changes in the codebase. These documents would include contribution guidelines, licensing information, community guidelines, and explanations regarding the platform’s structure and features. Keeping documentation up to date helps contributors understand the project and reduces confusion when making updates.
Another method BetterMotherhood plans to use to manage technical debt involves carefully reviewing all contributions before they are merged into the main codebase. Coding guidelines would be established and enforced to ensure consistency across the platform. BetterMotherhood would also regularly check in with contributors and organize workshops to periodically remind them of contribution processes and coding standards. Contributors would be encouraged to ask for help when needed, which promotes accountability and collaboration among all contributors.
BetterMotherhood also promotes sustainability by using agile development practices. The platform would focus on releasing updates iteratively in smaller sections rather than making large changes all at once. Incremental updates allow issues to be addressed early before they become larger problems. Simple bug fixes and small improvements to the user experience would be prioritized instead of delaying changes until issues become harder to manage.
Finally, sustainability depends heavily on community involvement and shared responsibility. As an open-source project, contributors play an important role in identifying bugs, suggesting areas for improvement, and maintaining existing features. These responsibilities would be distributed among contributors to ensure fair practices. By encouraging shared ownership and collaboration, BetterMotherhood can reduce the technical debt it accumulates while supporting long-term sustainability.

## Licensing, Data Law, and Privacy:
Licensing, data protection, and privacy are important aspects of BetterMotherhood, particularly because the platform is centered around sensitive topics related to maternal health and personal experiences. To support and encourage transparency, collaboration, and ethical use, BetterMotherhood would operate under an open-source license while also prioritizing user privacy and responsible data handling.
BetterMotherhood would make use of the MIT License. This license allows others to view, use, modify, and contribute to the platform’s source code, ensuring that the platform remains open and accessible. Using the MIT License, along with other open-source practices, helps ensure that the project remains transparent and structured in a way that does not harm community trust.
Because BetterMotherhood is built around health-related discussions, it is important that the platform considers and respects relevant data laws. While the platform is not intended to function as a medical provider or store official medical records, it still acknowledges the sensitive nature of maternal health information. As a result, the platform would not allow users to share experiences that are not their own unless consent has been given. BetterMotherhood would also avoid collecting protected health information that users do not consent to share. User-submitted content would be accepted only when it is shared voluntarily. Overall, the platform would maintain a general awareness of health-related data protection standards, such as HIPAA, when designing and managing the system.
The platform would allow users to share accurate and relevant information either anonymously or non-anonymously. This reduces fear and allows users to share their experiences without concern of retaliation, as BetterMotherhood is intended to be a safe and supportive space where Black women can openly discuss their experiences. The platform would collect minimal personal data and clearly inform users about how their data is used. Consent would be a main priority, and users would have control over what they share, including the ability to request content removal. These measures are put in place to prevent mishandling of user data.

## Community Governance:
Community governance plays an important role in ensuring that BetterMotherhood operates in a transparent and fair manner. The platform is built around sensitive topics such as medical racism and Black maternal mortality, which makes it essential to establish clear guidelines and structures that support decision-making and encourage accountability.
BetterMotherhood will be open for anyone to join; however, there will be a small group of moderators and platform maintainers. These individuals will be responsible for reviewing contributions, overseeing platform activity, and ensuring that the project’s vision, goals, and values remain prioritized. Platform maintainers will be tasked with approving code before it is merged into the GitHub repository, while moderators will focus on reviewing user-submitted content to ensure it aligns with platform rules and that all users are treated respectfully.
BetterMotherhood recognizes that concentrating these responsibilities among a select group may feel unfair, so elections will be held to select moderators and maintainers. This allows community members to have a voice in leadership decisions. Additionally, all decisions, whether major or minor, will be documented and communicated transparently. These documents will be publicly accessible so contributors can understand how decisions are made. Contribution guidelines and governance policies will also be publicly available to ensure clarity around expectations and processes.
While the platform is open to everyone, BetterMotherhood is primarily designed to serve Black women. As a result, Black women will be prioritized in leadership and moderation roles, as they are most directly impacted by the issues the platform addresses. This approach helps ensure that decisions are informed by lived experiences and that Black voices remain centered within the community.
Overall, the governance structure of BetterMotherhood is designed to promote accountability, fairness, and transparency. Clearly defined roles and open communication help maintain a safe and supportive environment while staying aligned with the platform’s mission and values.

## Community Engagement:
Community engagement is a core aspect of BetterMotherhood, as the platform is designed to be a space where users can share experiences, support one another, and build connections within the community. Because of the sensitive nature of the topics being discussed, it is important that BetterMotherhood remains in tune with the community and ensures that conversations are respectful, intentional, and supportive.
BetterMotherhood encourages users to participate in community engagement by sharing their personal experiences, resources, and advice related to maternal health and navigating motherhood. As mentioned earlier, users are welcome to participate in discussions either anonymously or non-anonymously. Users can create posts, start or contribute to discussions, and support one another through shared experiences.
In addition to online engagement, BetterMotherhood plans to extend conversations into real-life spaces by hosting seminars around the country focused on maternal health and medical racism. These seminars aim to raise awareness and educate individuals about their options when navigating these issues. While in-person events are valuable, maintaining an online community is equally important so that individuals who may not be able to attend seminars in person can still participate. This approach helps promote accessibility and inclusion.
BetterMotherhood will also establish clear community guidelines that play an important role in shaping engagement on the platform. These guidelines encourage respect, empathy, and active listening. Online seminars and resources would also be provided to help community members learn how to respond to sensitive topics appropriately. Disrespectful or harmful behavior would not be tolerated, ensuring that the platform remains a safe and supportive environment.

The next steps for BetterMotherhood focus on improvement, growth, and reaching a broader audience. One potential next step involves expanding, iterating, and further developing the platform based on user feedback received as the platform is rolled out. As more users begin to engage with BetterMotherhood, the insights gained can be used to determine what updates should be made and what ideas may be suitable for future expansion.
Additionally, BetterMotherhood would be open to exploring potential partnerships with maternal health and motherhood organizations that are actively addressing issues related to medical racism and maternal mortality. These partnerships would be vital in helping the platform achieve its goals and reach its full potential. Collaborations with organizations that already work in this space can also help strengthen outreach, credibility, and impact.
To further encourage participation, BetterMotherhood may introduce incentives for contributors and partners to motivate individuals to share resources, experiences, and technical contributions to the platform. These incentives could help sustain engagement and encourage continued involvement from the community.
Finally, BetterMotherhood remains open to the possibility of expanding to support other marginalized groups facing similar healthcare disparities, while still maintaining a strong focus on Black women. Overall, the next steps for BetterMotherhood emphasize continuous iteration, collaboration, and sustainable growth of the platform.

## Conclusion:
Black maternal mortality and medical racism remain persistent issues within the United States healthcare system. Addressing these challenges requires comprehensive solutions that promote transparency and accountability, which is why BetterMotherhood is being proposed. While BetterMotherhood is only a starting point, the platform is intended to serve as a resource for anyone who needs guidance, helping users understand their next steps and how to navigate maternal healthcare systems.
This hypothetical open-source platform aims to address these challenges by centering Black women’s experiences and providing accessible resources to help users navigate maternal health and related issues.
